PART 2: UNDERSTANDING AWARENESS LEVELS
Before you write anything, you need to know WHERE your audience is mentally.
Level What They Know What They Need
Unaware Don't know they have a problem Education on the problem
Problem Aware Know they have a problem, don't know solutions exist Show them solutions exist
Solution Aware Know solutions exist, don't know our product Show them why our product is best
Product Aware Know our product, not convinced yet Overcome objections, build trust
Most Aware Ready to buy Just need the offer
Why This Matters:
Most of our ads educate people on the problem (parasites, gut dysbiosis, etc.). That's Problem Aware and
Unaware targeting. But Solution Aware is low-hanging fruit. These people already KNOW solutions exist.
They've tried stuff. They're actively looking. They just don't know Resilia is the best option yet. This is easier
conversion because we don't need to educate them on the problem - we just need to show them why Resilia is
better than what they've tried.

PART 4: THINKING IN LAYERS
This is the most important part. When you find a market, you need to think in LAYERS.
EXAMPLE: WEIGHT LOSS MARKET
LAYER 1: THE PROBLEM (huge market)
Person is fat / can't lose weight
LAYER 2: WHAT THEY BELIEVE IS CAUSING IT
- Slow metabolism - Hormonal imbalance (thyroid, estrogen, cortisol) - Insulin resistance - Genetics - Age

it's slow..." (Building on their belief = easier sell, feels like revelation)

PART 8: ANALYZING COMPETITOR ADS
When you find a winning ad from another brand, break it down like this:
What to Identify:
UMP (Unique Mechanism of the Problem): - What do they say is the ROOT CAUSE? - How do they explain
WHY the problem exists? UMS (Unique Mechanism of the Solution): - What does their product do
differently? - Why does their solution work when others don't? Structure: - How do they open? - How do they
build the problem? - How do they introduce the solution? - How do they close?
Example Breakdown - Vitacore Kidney Ad:
UMP: - Nephrons (kidney filters) are clogged with toxins - Water doesn't fix it - it just flows around the clog -
Clogged filters = protein leaks = foamy urine - Clogged filters = toxins build up = exhaustion - Clogged filters =
inflammation = lower back pain UMS: - Fulvic acid (from Shilajit) penetrates nephron cells - Binds to toxins and
flushes them out - Heavy metals, uric acid, creatinine = gone What makes it work: - Simple mechanism:
clogged = penetrate = clear - Each step makes logical sense - Feels scientific but easy to understand How we
adapted it: - Kept same structure - Changed 'penetrates and binds' to 'breaks down toxic waste and calms
inflammation' - Carvacrol clears the buildup causing the clog - Thymoquinone calms kidney inflammation so
nephrons filter properly
Adaptation Process:
STEP 1: Identify the structure (hook, mechanism, product intro, proof, CTA) STEP 2: Keep what's working
(format, emotional beats, pacing, CTA energy) STEP 3: Replace product-specific elements (name, format,
ingredients, mechanism, benefits, stats, offer) STEP 4: Adjust language (remove jargon, simplify, match our
tone, add texture) STEP 5: Check compliance (soften claims, add qualifiers)
PART 9: THE 'FEEL TRUE' TEST
Not everything needs to be perfectly scientifically proven. It needs to FEEL true to the audience.
Questions to Ask:
1. Does this make intuitive sense? 2. Would the audience nod along when they hear this? 3. Is there SOME
truth to it, even if we're stretching? 4. Does it connect dots they haven't connected before?
Example - Lymphatic System (Sculptique):
Sculptique sells a hemorrhoid product and claims 'lymphatic drainage' is the root cause. Is it perfectly true?
Not really. Hemorrhoids are a VEIN problem, not lymphatic. Does it FEEL true? Yes. 'Drainage' and
'inflammation' make intuitive sense for hemorrhoids. Do their ingredients actually work? Yes - Horse
Chestnut is studied for hemorrhoids. Conclusion: They've rebranded a vein issue as a 'lymphatic' issue for
marketing. The ingredients work, but the mechanism is dressed up. INSIGHT: If they can stretch it, we can
stretch it too - as long as the underlying ingredients actually do something relevant.
The Spectrum of Claims:
Claim Type Example Risk Level
Directly proven Carvacrol kills bacteria Low
Strongly supported Black seed oil supports kidney function Low
Reasonable connection Gut health affects skin Medium
Intuitive stretch Parasites cause slow metabolism Medium
Marketing reframe Lymphatic drainage causes hemorrhoids Medium-High
Complete fabrication Cures cancer DO NOT USE

PART 13: SUMMARY - THE THINKING PROCESS
When approaching any new angle, ask:
1. Who is the audience? (Demographics + beliefs) 2. What do they currently believe is causing their
problem? 3. What are they already buying to solve it? 4. What's the deeper WHY that connects to our
product? 5. Can we validate their belief and go deeper, or do we need to reframe entirely? 6. What's our
unique mechanism? (How specifically does our product fix the deeper cause?) 7. Is this factually true
enough? Does it FEEL true to the audience? 8. What structure/format works best for this audience? 9. What
tone do they respond to?
